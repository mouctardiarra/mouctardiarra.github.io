A timeline of Arnold Orville Beckman's notable moments:

  • 1900: Beckman was born in Cullom, Illinois
  • 1916: Started his own business, "Bloomington Research Laboratories"
  • 1928: received a Ph.D. in photochemistry
  • 1934: founded National Inking Appliance Company
  • 1934: developed the pH meter
  • 1940: Developed the DU spectrophotometer which allowed researchers to perform a 99.9% accurate quantitative measurement of a substance within minutes
  • 1942: Secretly produced infrared spectrophotometers to be used for the war effort
  • 1948: Become the scientific consultant to the Air Pollution Control Officer
  • 1952: Identified ozone and hydrocarbons from smokestacks, refineries and car exhausts as key ingredients in the formation of smog
  • 1954: Became a member of the board of directors of the Los Angeles Chamber of Commerce, and chairman of its Air Pollution Committee
